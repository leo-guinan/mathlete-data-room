class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Introduction</strong></p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="e7d5" name="e7d5">So what I do I mean that our relationship with time is unhealthy? I base this off of what I have learned about myself recently. I realized that I wasn’t looking at time the right way. I was viewing time through the same lens that everyone else does, or at least that I thought everyone else did. The more I read about the success stories of others, I realized that I saw more than a few common ideas. But I also noted that those common ideas rarely occurred in the same time frame in peoples’ lives. Some gained success quickly, with little struggle (or at least <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">seemingly </em>little struggle). Others fight for a long time, eventually becoming successful.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="414b" name="414b">This led me to a big Idea. Time is relative to everyone. My time is not the same as your time. My past is not the same as your past. My present is not the same as your present. And my futures are not the same as your futures.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing" id="e8e6" name="e8e6">In this series, I will be looking at each of the parts of time. I will examine my own personal relationship to them, and how I think that might apply to everyone else. I hope to help others understand that they have more control in their lives than they realize. But in order to control your futures, you need to understand how you relate to time.</p></div></div></section>
