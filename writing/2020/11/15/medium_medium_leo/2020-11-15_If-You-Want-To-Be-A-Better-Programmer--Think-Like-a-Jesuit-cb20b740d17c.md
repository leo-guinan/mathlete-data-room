episode.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="98fa" name="98fa">In very simple terms, casuistry is looking at a specific case only on the facts of that case alone. Then you can apply that specific case to unrelated cases.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="164d" name="164d">To start, let’s see what happens when we do the opposite.</p><h4 class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p" id="258c" name="258c">My Story</h4><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4" id="c6d5" name="c6d5">I graduated with a BS in Computer Science. My first job was writing primarily Java. As such, I learned a lot about <a class="markup--anchor markup--p-anchor" data-href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Object-oriented_programming" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Object-Oriented programming</a>.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="b218" name="b218">Object-Oriented Programming has a few key ideas:</p><ul class="postList"><li class="graf graf--li graf-after--p" id="d5de" name="d5de">Encapsulation — put your objects in a box to protect them from the outside world and the outside world from them.</li><li class="graf graf--li graf-after--li" id="a982" name="a982">Polymorphism — some things are more than one thing.</li><li class="graf graf--li graf-after--li" id="fc52" name="fc52">Inversion-of-control or dependency injection —passing objects in instead of building them within the calling object</li><li class="graf graf--li graf-after--li" id="5d7a" name="5d7a">henry (this was added by my 5yr old son, Henry. He was so proud of it, I decided to leave it in.)</li><li class="graf graf--li graf-after--li" id="e8b2" name="e8b2">composition vs. inheritance — don’t use inheritance just to use inheritance. Typically, composition ends up being more flexible and is easier to test in my experience.</li></ul><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--li" id="14b8" name="14b8">I had these ideas drilled into my head. I knew them backward and forward.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="c172" name="c172">Until I stopped writing Object Oriented code. I went back and forth between backend Java and frontend Javascript. Then I ended up switching to Python.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="0b6c" name="0b6c">Python uses objects. But it is more functional in nature. I ended up writing much more functional Python.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="700e" name="700e">The change in my career went slowly, over ten years. I ended up shifting my entire mindset over to functional programming. I used Python classes, but I did so in a functional manner.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="e945" name="e945">Until one day it clicked for me. I realized that I had been so focused on what I was doing, I lost track of the things I had previously learned.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="f528" name="f528">So what does this have to do with casuistry?</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="6be3" name="6be3">Everything. I realized that I was looking at programming as a top-down approach. I picked my toolkit that I would use based on the attributes of the programming language I was using and not the specific problem I was facing. If I was writing Java, I would write object-oriented code. If I was writing Python, I wrote functional code.</p><h4 class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p" id="d47a" name="d47a">Concepts vs Problem Solving</h4><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4" id="2aec" name="2aec">I believe this has a lot to do with how we tend to teach concepts. We give a broad concept and then show how to narrow it down to a specific problem.</p><blockquote class="graf graf--pullquote graf-after--p" id="8750" name="8750">If you are writing Java, follow these object-oriented principles.</blockquote><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--pullquote" id="34f3" name="34f3">But that isn’t why these principles exist. They don’t exist because we are writing object-oriented code. They exist because they are the best way to solve certain problems.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="c53e" name="c53e">So flip it. Let’s stop thinking about programming design principles in terms of functional vs object-oriented. Let’s look at specific problems, and determine what the best solution is.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="097e" name="097e">My realization was this. I started using objects in Python to encapsulate certain bits of logic. There were some trouble areas in the code that I was trying to tighten up so I could figure out the issues. Had I taken the time to do this at the beginning of the project, my life would have been much easier.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p graf--trailing" id="fe9b" name="fe9b">I should have known I was down the wrong path. There were signs everywhere, particularly the fact that the system was hard to test the way I wanted to. I was just too attached to this separation I had in my head between object-oriented and functional programming.</p></div></div></section><section class="section section--body section--last" name="25dc"><div class="section-divider"><hr class="section-divider"/></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><h4 class="graf graf--h4 graf--leading" id="7386" name="7386">My Advice Is This</h4><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4" id="450b" name="450b">Forget all the rules you learned about programming. Look at the problem you are trying to solve. Break that problem down into the smallest sub-problems you can. Then solve one at a time. Forget about the big picture. Focus on solving the small problems you can easily solve. When you solve enough of the small problems, the solution to the big problem becomes obvious.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="7a5c" name="7a5c">Once you are done, look at the code again. See what patterns start to show up. For me personally, the light came on when I started doing just that.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="329a" name="329a">I let my experience drive my focus. I got arrogant in my knowledge and thought I could solve bigger problems without breaking them down.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="be64" name="be64">My ego led to bugs. I had to throw out my ego and revisit the problem as a beginner. I started with the basic idea of what I wanted to build and then started building it a small piece at a time.</p><blockquote class="graf graf--pullquote graf-after--p" id="6d82" name="6d82">When I focused on the problem I was solving, and not my knowledge, the problem became much easier.</blockquote><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--pullquote" id="49c4" name="49c4"><a class="markup--anchor
