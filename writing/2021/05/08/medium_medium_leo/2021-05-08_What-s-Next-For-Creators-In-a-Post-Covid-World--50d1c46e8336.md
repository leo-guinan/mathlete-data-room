<section class="section section--body section--first" name="9ce9"><div class="section-divider"><hr class="section-divider"/></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><h3 class="graf graf--h3 graf--leading graf--title" id="0ed5" name="0ed5">What’s Next For Creators In a Post-Covid World?</h3><h4 class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--h3 graf--subtitle" id="44c8" name="44c8">Art Is Eternal, Life Is Fleeting</h4></div><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--fullWidth"><figure class="graf graf--figure graf--layoutFillWidth graf-after--h4" id="db94" name="db94"><img class="graf-image" data-height="2250" data-image-id="0*-eiQ870ADQi74Nz4" data-is-featured="true" data-unsplash-photo-id="Tjio9DgtIls" data-width="4000" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/2560/0*-eiQ870ADQi74Nz4"/><figcaption class="imageCaption">Photo by <a class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" data-href="https://unsplash.com/@joshtw?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" href="https://unsplash.com/@joshtw?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" rel="photo-creator noopener" target="_blank">Josh Liu</a> on <a class="markup--anchor markup--figure-anchor" data-href="https://unsplash.com?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" href="https://unsplash.com?utm_source=medium&amp;utm_medium=referral" rel="photo-source noopener" target="_blank">Unsplash</a></figcaption></figure></div><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure" id="54fb" name="54fb">If you’re like me, you are itching to get back out into the world as soon as you can. It’s weird, I didn’t go out much before the pandemic, but now I’m just looking forward to the day when I can go out to places like bars without thinking about the word “pandemic”.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="698a" name="698a">When the pandemic first hit, I thought I was living my best life. As an introvert, I suddenly found myself with more time than I knew what to do with. I was way more productive in my work. I was finally able to spend more time doing the things I loved to do. I discovered that I really enjoy writing. It gave me a creative outlet that I didn’t realize I needed.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="29ee" name="29ee">It seems like others have also discovered creative outlets. The rise of the creator economy is proof of that fact. People are figuring out how to create art and profit from it in a digital environment. The metaverse is where many people have started living and working; the digital universe that now contains some of the most expensive art in the world.</p><blockquote class="graf graf--pullquote graf-after--p" id="9a19" name="9a19"><strong class="markup--strong markup--pullquote-strong">Ars Longa, Vita Brevis — Art is Eternal, Life is Fleeting</strong></blockquote><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--pullquote" id="6c1b" name="6c1b">But what happens when the world no longer has to live digitally?</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="1fe3" name="1fe3">How do artists continue to continue to connect with their audiences when people start venturing back into the real world? Sure, some will be fine. Those with huge followings will not have an issue. But what about the smaller creators?</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="b123" name="b123">I predict that many smaller creators will struggle initially. Their audience has limited entertainment options and creators have filled in for the lack of in-person entertainment. But once everything reopens and the world starts to shift back to primarily real-world interactions, creators will need to adapt.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="8461" name="8461">But what if you could get your digital art displayed in physical locations? What if you could place your art in places that your audience will be going? This opens creators to a new world of possibilities.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="5e38" name="5e38">Imagine leaving digital graffiti in local bars that only your followers can see. Or maybe you want to display your art on the wall of the bar (or a whole bunch of bars) leading up to the NFT auction?</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="464a" name="464a">Maybe a bar owner could partner with influencers and creators to create a digital scavenger hunt? Bring people back in and help artists benefit. The possibilities are endless.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="fe63" name="fe63">And the idea isn’t limited to bars. Any physical venue could participate. When we start mixing the physical with the virtual, magical things can start to happen. The limit is your imagination.</p><p class="graf graf--p graf-after--p" id="1732" name="1732"><strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong">Ars Longa, Vita Brevis — Art is Eternal, Life is Fleeting</strong></p><figure class="graf graf--figure graf--iframe graf-after--p graf--trailing" id="fff8" name="fff8"><iframe frameborder="0" height="393" scrolling="no" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/jKw5AxBbSjA?feature=oembed" width="700"></iframe></figure></div></div></section><section class="section section--body section--last" name="d5b4"><div class="section-divider"><hr class="section-divider"/></div><div class="section-content"><div class="section-inner sectionLayout--insetColumn"><p class="graf graf--p graf--leading graf--trailing" id="c9cb" name="c9cb">Thanks for reading!
