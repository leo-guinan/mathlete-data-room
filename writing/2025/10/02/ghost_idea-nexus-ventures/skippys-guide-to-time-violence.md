And once you see it, you can't unsee it.</p><p>Let's begin.</p><hr><h2 id="part-1-what-is-time-violence">PART 1: WHAT IS TIME VIOLENCE?</h2><h3 id="the-definition">The Definition</h3><p><strong>Time Violence (TV)</strong> is the systematic theft of human time through unnecessary complexity.</p><p>It's the gap between:</p><ul><li>How long something SHOULD take (optimal)</li><li>How long it ACTUALLY takes (reality)</li></ul><p>When that gap exceeds <strong>10x</strong>, it's violence.<br>When it exceeds <strong>100x</strong>, it's criminal.</p><h3 id="the-formula">The Formula</h3><pre><code>TV(S) = Ops_Score(S) × (1 + Info_Score(S))
</code></pre><p><strong>Where:</strong></p><p><strong>Ops_Score(S)</strong> = Operational waste (waiting, repeating, navigating)</p><pre><code>Ops_Score = (ρ/(1-ρ)) × ((c²_arrival + c²_service)/2) × τ
</code></pre><ul><li>ρ = system utilization (how overloaded it is)</li><li>c = coefficient of variation (how unpredictable wait times are)</li><li>τ = mean service time (how long each interaction takes)</li></ul><p><strong>Info_Score(S)</strong> = Information waste (confusion, redundancy, cognitive load)</p><pre><code>Info_Score = D_KL(P_actual || P_minimal) + H(decisions) + I(redundancy)
</code></pre><ul><li>D_KL = Kullback-Leibler divergence (how far from minimal complexity)</li><li>H = Entropy of decisions (how many unclear choices)</li><li>I = Information redundancy (how much you repeat yourself)</li></ul><h3 id="translation-for-monkeys">Translation for Monkeys</h3><p>You don't need to calculate the exact formula (yet).</p><p>You just need to understand:</p><p><strong>Time Violence = How long it takes × How unnecessarily complex it is</strong></p><hr><h2 id="part-2-the-three-types-of-time-violence">PART 2: THE THREE TYPES OF TIME VIOLENCE</h2><h3 id="type-1-operational-violence">TYPE 1: OPERATIONAL VIOLENCE</h3><p><strong>Definition:</strong> You waste time WAITING, REPEATING, or NAVIGATING.</p><p><strong>Examples:</strong></p><ul><li>Waiting on hold for customer service (30+ minutes)</li><li>Re-entering the same information across multiple forms</li><li>Calling 5 different departments to get one answer</li><li>Driving to 3 locations because systems don't talk to each other</li></ul><p><strong>How to Calculate:</strong></p><pre><code>Ops_Score = Hours Wasted × Repetition Factor

Example:
- Task: Filing insurance claim
- Optimal time: 15 minutes
- Actual time: 4 hours (multiple calls, re-submissions)
- Repetition: Done 3 times due to "errors"

Ops_Score = 4 hours × 3 = 12 hours of pure waste
</code></pre><p><strong>The Violence:</strong> You'll never get those 12 hours back.</p><hr><h3 id="type-2-information-violence">TYPE 2: INFORMATION VIOLENCE</h3><p><strong>Definition:</strong> You waste cognitive energy on CONFUSION, DECISIONS, or REDUNDANCY.</p><p><strong>Examples:</strong></p><ul><li>Forms that ask for information they already have</li><li>Unclear instructions that force you to guess</li><li>47 options when 3 would suffice</li><li>Jargon designed to confuse rather than clarify</li></ul><p><strong>How to Calculate:</strong></p><pre><code>Info_Score = Mental Load × Unnecessary Complexity

Example:
- Task: Understanding your health insurance coverage
- Optimal clarity: "You pay $X, we cover $Y"
- Actual clarity: 47-page PDF with contradictory clauses
- Decision points: 23 unclear choices about coverage

Info_Score = 23 decisions × 2 hours research each = 46 hours
</code></pre><p><strong>The Violence:</strong> Even after 46 hours, you STILL don't know what's covered.</p><hr><h3 id="type-3-combined-violence">TYPE 3: COMBINED VIOLENCE</h3><p><strong>Definition:</strong> Systems that hit you with BOTH operational AND information waste simultaneously.</p><p><strong>Examples:</strong></p><ul><li>College financial aid (FAFSA): Confusing forms + multiple submissions + unclear deadlines</li><li>Disability benefits: Requires medical records you don't have + 6-month wait + confusing criteria</li><li>Legal system: Unclear procedures + multiple filings + court delays</li></ul><p><strong>How to Calculate:</strong></p><pre><code>TV(S) = Ops_Score × (1 + Info_Score)

Example: Applying for disability benefits
- Ops_Score: 200 hours (gathering records, waiting, resubmitting)
- Info_Score: 1.5 (50% more complex than it needs to be)

TV(S) = 200 × (1 + 1.5) = 500 hours of Time Violence
</code></pre><p><strong>The Violence:</strong> 500 hours = 12.5 weeks of full-time work. For ONE application.</p><hr><h2 id="part-3-time-violence-in-your-life">PART 3: TIME VIOLENCE IN YOUR LIFE</h2><h3 id="the-self-audit">The Self-Audit</h3><p>Most monkeys don't realize they're experiencing Time Violence because it feels "normal."</p><p>Let's fix that.</p><p><strong>EXERCISE 1: Identify Your Time Violence</strong></p><p>List every system you interact with regularly:</p><ul><li>[ ] Healthcare (insurance, doctors, prescriptions)</li><li>[ ] Government (taxes, benefits, permits)</li><li>[ ] Employment (job searching, applications, HR)</li><li>[ ] Education (applications, financial aid, registration)</li><li>[ ] Legal (contracts, disputes, filings)</li><li>[ ] Financial (banking, loans, credit)</li><li>[ ] Housing (rentals, mortgages, utilities)</li></ul><p>For EACH system, ask:</p><ol><li>How long does it ACTUALLY take?</li><li>How long SHOULD it take?</li><li>What's the ratio?
